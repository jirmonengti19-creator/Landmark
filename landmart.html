Legal Help & Paperwork Guide

Everything you need to know about property transfer paperwork in India — step by step.

1

Agreement to Sell

Buyer and seller sign a written agreement mentioning price, payment terms, and possession date. Usually done on ₹100–₹500 stamp paper drafted by a lawyer.

⏱ 1–2 days  |  💰 ₹2,000–₹5,000 lawyer fee
2

Due Diligence

Verify the seller truly owns the property. Get an Encumbrance Certificate from the Sub-Registrar office and check the 7/12 extract (land) or Khata document to confirm no loans or disputes exist.

⏱ 3–7 days  |  💰 ₹500–₹1,000
3

Sale Deed Drafting

The main legal document that transfers ownership. Must be drafted by a registered lawyer and printed on stamp paper valued at a percentage of the property price.

⏱ 2–3 days  |  💰 ₹5,000–₹15,000 lawyer fee
4

Registration at Sub-Registrar Office

Both buyer and seller visit the Sub-Registrar Office with 2 witnesses. Pay stamp duty (5–7%) and registration fee (~1%) of the property value. Document gets officially registered. ✅

⏱ 1 day visit  |  💰 6–8% of property value
5

Mutation of Property

Update land records in the buyer's name at the local municipal office or Tehsil. This officially makes you the owner in government records.

⏱ 2–4 weeks  |  💰 ₹200–₹500

📄 Documents Checklist

👤 Both Buyer & Seller
  • ✅ Aadhar Card (original + copy)
  • ✅ PAN Card
  • ✅ 2 Passport Size Photos
  • ✅ Address Proof
🏠 Seller Must Provide
  • ✅ Original Title Deed
  • ✅ Encumbrance Certificate
  • ✅ Property Tax Receipts
  • ✅ No Objection Certificate (if applicable)
  • ✅ 7/12 Extract or Khata (for land)
